Chapter III hospitals and patients

Section 1 Why do humans need hospitals
There are three realms of clinical medical assistance. The first realm is treatment, focusing on the survival of the body and technical intervention; The second realm is salvation, which focuses on both body and mind, and emotional care; The third realm is redemption, focusing on the integration of subject and object, and thoroughly understanding and sublimation. Understand what? “Meet Tuesday” tells us that the hospital is the last classroom of life. Here, we have a thorough understanding of the true meaning of life.
Life is just a journey, and the body cannot last forever
Death is the return of physical life, and the spirit (love) is eternal
To live to death, short life is meaningful

Section 2 what’s wrong with patients?
In English, there is a difference between “disease” and “illness”: a disease is a clinical judgment that deviates from the normal (healthy) state based on the specific etiology, special symptoms, and positive indications detected by laboratories and various modern medical instruments. The patient is the painful experience and physical and mental experience that the disease individual tells.
Professor Arthur Kleinman, a medical psychologist and sociologist at Harvard University, believes that “disease” and “illness” are two different worlds: a world of doctors and a world of patients; One is the world observed and recorded, and the other is the world experienced and described; One is to find the objective world of etiology and pathological indicators, and the other is to tell the subjective world of psychological and social painful experience.

Therefore, the “greed for life and fear of death” in the realistic context is a social consciousness bred in the soil of technicism and consumerism.
