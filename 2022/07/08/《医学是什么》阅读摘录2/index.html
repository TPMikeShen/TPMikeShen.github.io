《医学是什么》阅读摘录2

Posted on 2022-07-08

What is medicine?

Text

Chapter 1 Medicine: Science? Human studies?

Section 1 examination of “medical registration”

Overemphasizing scientificity and scientization may narrow the academic vision of medicine, which is the so-called “cover of modernity” or “loss of Scientism”.
In this era of “science” worship. Science, as a knowledge system, has long been ideologized and plated with a too solemn aura. Therefore, the title of science is too much of a stake for the professional group of Medicine - to say who is not science implies a kind of dualistic exclusion thinking. If you are not a scientist, you must be non science, pseudoscience and anti science. This is a fatal “subversion” of professional identity.
What is the cost of one-sided scientization and technicalization of medicine? The price is the loss of medical humanism and the order of medical economy. Consumerism prevails. We seem to be used to naming patients with numbers that lack warmth. 35 beds, 42 specimens… Human suffering suddenly turns into cold pathological sections and images; In the hospital, there are rooms full of machines, plus expressionless strange faces; On the one hand, there is a serious shortage of health resources, on the other hand, there is extreme extravagance and waste.
Another philosopher who thinks in disease, tooms, pointed out sharply in the meaning of patients: “doctor, you are just observing, and I am experiencing”. She believes that there are “two worlds” in the process of patients. What I experience is pain (the patient’s world), and what you observe is disease (the doctor’s world). Disease and pain are not the same thing. The focus of your observation is disease rather than people, and medicine has forgotten “people”.
In fact, there are four types of “treatment” in the professional context: etiological treatment, etiological treatment, symptomatic treatment, and placebo treatment.
Trudeau, a famous doctor in the United States, has a famous saying: “sometimes to cure, often to care, always to comfort”. He engraved this sentence on his tombstone.
Musen: “although the cognitive content of medicine may be restored to biology, even physics and chemistry, medicine, as a whole discipline, cannot be restored to biology.”
Because its scientificity is in the “entanglement” of Humanities and social science knowledge and methods, medicine is no longer just a special technology, a tree of knowledge, a career to make a living, but a career and intellectual life full of pleasure.
The second section questions visualism and objectivism
Its cognitive root is the objectification, objectification and reductionist thinking of life, which obscures the subjectivity and experience of life activities; Its fatal injury is indifference to painful experience; The unbearable pain is the temperature of losing humanity in the process of diagnosis and treatment.
Section III “spiritual black hole” of medicine in the 20th century
In fact, this idea that science is naturally clean is harmful. Science does not have the characteristics of natural cleanliness and the function of self moral purification.
Section IV frontier technology faces serious ethical impact
Everyone is immersed in the “drama” of scientific development, competition, conquest, coronation and carnival. Everyone is competing. What is the result of the competition? This technology may completely erase the contingency and diversity of human reproduction. If cloning becomes a routine, there will be no contingency.
The consequences of this intervention in life will inevitably lead to the disorder of human relations and biological genealogy.
Section V tension between “ought to be” and “necessity”
Section 6 what is traditional Chinese medicine?
Traditional Chinese Medicine - because first it is effective, then it is reasonable. It can be effective and unreasonable, of course, it can also be effective and reasonable.
At present, the mission of Chinese culture is to return to its roots and create a new one: to return to its roots and revitalize the confidence of national culture; We should also open up a new era and embark on the journey of cultural innovation, and maintain the necessary tension between the two.
In the context of the intersection and dialogue between Chinese and Western painting, Mr. Pan Tianshou, a giant of traditional Chinese painting, has the wisdom of “drawing a distance between Chinese and Western painting”. This argument is also of enlightening significance to the future development of traditional Chinese medicine.
According to the inertial deduction of its survival and development in the 20th century, the evolution of traditional Chinese medicine has roughly three ways: first, being willing to live in the second class and continue to become complementary / alternative medicine; The second is to devote life experience, early clinical experience, research inspiration and materials to scientific and technological medicine from the west, and become a hypothesis base to be verified; Third, the full publicity of academic subjectivity has made it a Chinese type of medicine, catching up with and surpassing Western types of medicine in some fields (sub-health care, elderly diseases, chronic diseases, disability diagnosis and treatment), and creating new treatment characteristics and market patterns.
