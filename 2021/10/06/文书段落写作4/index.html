文书段落写作4

Posted on 2021-10-06

Every person has a creative side, and it can be expressed in many ways: problem solving, original and innovative thinking, and artistically, to name a few. Describe how you express your creative side. (Maximum 350 words)

When I was in the sixth grade of primary school, I took part in a printmaking competition called “Lotus Race”. The purpose of the competition was to choose excellent works of art based on their originality from primary school pupils and exhibit them in an art museum near the Nanjing Library.

I was encouraged to take part by my art teacher since I was a member of the printmaking club in our school. In addition, I was also interested in drawing at that time, so I decided to have a try.

In preparation for the first draft, I drew with a 6B pencil which had a darker color for leaving a mark on the sponge paper under the draft and a piece of 4K paper which was about twice the size of A4 paper. At the start of the process, I was not sure what to draw however, I suddenly came up with a good idea which was to draw a robot. In 2015, robots seemed to be gradually appearing in people’s lives, used for activities such as sweeping, guiding the general public to various destinations like in Nanjing and robots who help in the kitchen. I thought that they could help people in many ways as fabulous assistants since I wanted to create more opportunities for robots to help humans, so I focused on firefighting. It was not an unrealistic idea as I discovered that forest fires occurred frequently because of dry weather in various areas of China. If there was a robot to help, it could be easier since the robots could be controlled remotely and firefighters could avoid the dangers of being close to fierce fires.

After some creative thinking, my first draft of the design was finished. The robot had a big square head for scanning and patrolling, eight long legs with wheels for fast movement and the fire extinguishing system in its body. Although I was still young, which meant some details were not precise enough, I tried my best to make the scene look realistic by drawing a background which showed the robot putting out the fire of buildings in a busy city center with spray pipes.

Surprisingly, I won a prize and my work was shown in the exhibition. It was an impressed experience as it inspired me to use my creativity to contribute to the society in the future. Just as the saying goes “creativity comes from life.”

​ SHEN ZIYOU
